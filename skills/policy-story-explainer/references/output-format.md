# Output format

## Two tiers for long policies

Offer two tiers so readers can choose their depth:

1. **One-page executive summary** — the obligations, deadlines, and exceptions as a scannable
   bullet list, mandatory language intact. Enough to act on without reading the full version.
2. **Full story-led version** — the structure below, theme by theme.

Lead with the executive summary, then the full version. For a short policy, the full version alone
is fine. The executive summary restates the rules in plain English; it never drops a `must`/`shall`,
a threshold, or an exception to save space.

## Full-version template

Use this structure for the full version unless the user requests another format:

```md
# [Policy Name]: Story-led Explanation

## Executive summary

[For long policies only. The obligations, deadlines, and exceptions as scannable bullets,
mandatory language intact. Omit for a short policy.]

- [Obligation / deadline / threshold — `must`/`shall` and numbers unchanged.]
- [Another rule, with its section reference.]
- [Note any rule that has no stated exception.]

## Who this is for

[Describe the audience in plain English.]

## The policy in one paragraph

[Summarise the purpose of the policy without losing important meaning.]

---

## 1. [Theme or rule name]

### A realistic situation

[Describe a believable workplace, customer, school, operational, or personal scenario.]

### What the policy is saying

[Explain the rule clearly.]

### The actual policy point

[Quote or closely paraphrase the original rule. Include section reference if available.]

### What you should do

[Give practical actions based on the policy.]

### Why it matters

[Explain the risk, consequence, or reason behind the rule.]

---

## Important exceptions or edge cases

[List exceptions, approval routes, thresholds, or cases where the rule changes.]

## Things that may need clarification

[List unclear terms, missing examples, conflicts, or areas that require review.]

## Quick checklist

- [ ] [Action/check]
- [ ] [Action/check]
- [ ] [Action/check]
```
